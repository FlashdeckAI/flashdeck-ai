Our Sustainability Mission

FlashDeck.AI was built on a simple but powerful premise: the best revision tool is one that works better for students and better for the planet. Every year, millions of students around the world purchase index cards, revision guides, and printed materials that are used for a few weeks and then discarded. We believe there is a smarter way.

By replacing physical flashcards with AI-generated digital ones, FlashDeck eliminates paper waste, reduces stationery costs, and delivers a more effective revision experience — all at the same time. Sustainability is not an afterthought for us; it is built into the core purpose of the product.

The Environmental Impact of Paper Revision

The scale of paper consumption in student revision is significant. A typical GCSE student preparing for 10 subjects might purchase 500–1,000 index cards, multiple revision guides, and printed practice papers. Across the UK alone, millions of students sit GCSE and A Level exams each year — and the vast majority of that paper ends up in landfill or recycling within weeks of the exam.

Responsible AI

Artificial intelligence carries its own environmental footprint — training and running large language models requires significant computing power and energy. We take this responsibility seriously and have made deliberate choices to minimise our AI-related environmental impact:

  • We use Claude Haiku — Anthropic's most efficient model, optimised for speed and low energy consumption, rather than larger, more resource-intensive models.
  • Topic Bank caching — our architecture stores generated flashcard content and reuses it across users, meaning we make significantly fewer AI API calls than a system that generates fresh content for every request.
  • No unnecessary generation — we only call the AI when a user genuinely needs new content that does not already exist in our knowledge bank.

We will continue to monitor the energy efficiency of our AI providers and switch to greener alternatives as they become available.

Sustainability in Schools

When FlashDeck is deployed in a school, the environmental benefits multiply. A school of 500 students using FlashDeck across their GCSE revision could collectively avoid purchasing tens of thousands of index cards and hundreds of revision guides each academic year — representing a meaningful reduction in paper consumption and waste at the institutional level.

We encourage schools to include FlashDeck's adoption in their own sustainability reporting and environmental policies, and we are happy to provide data on estimated paper savings upon request.

As FlashDeck grows, we intend to deepen our sustainability commitments. Our roadmap includes:

We recognise that sustainability is a journey, not a destination. We will be honest about where we fall short and transparent about our progress.
